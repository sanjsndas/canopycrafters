Expert Tips & Advice

  • Always prune trees during dormant seasons to minimize stress and promote healthy growth.
  • Remove dead or diseased branches immediately to prevent spread of pests and illness.
  • Regularly inspect your trees for signs of pests, fungus, or nutrient deficiencies.
  • Use clean, sharp tools for all pruning to make clean cuts that heal faster.
  • Avoid excessive pruning which can weaken branches and increase vulnerability.
  • Mulch around the base of trees to retain moisture and regulate soil temperature.
  • Avoid piling soil or mulch against the trunk which can cause rot.
  • Water deeply and infrequently to encourage strong root systems.
  • Consult professionals for large pruning jobs to ensure safety and precision.
  • Protect trees from mechanical damage by installing guards or barriers as needed.
